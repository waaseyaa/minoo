## 5. History & Significance

- **Founded:** 1971 in Little Current, Manitoulin Island
- **Founders:** Earl Abotossaway, former Chief Jim McGregor, James D. Debassiage, Reverend Leonard Self, and Norman Debassiage
- **Original size:** 17 teams, ~200 players
- **Four pillars:** Education, Citizenship, Sportsmanship, Respect
- **Girls' division added:** 1999 (started with 5 teams, now 59)
- **Cultural significance:** Created to counteract racism Indigenous youth faced in mainstream hockey leagues. Provides a safe, supportive environment for First Nations youth.
- **Notable alumni:** Ted Nolan (Ojibwe) — played 78 NHL games with Detroit Red Wings and Pittsburgh Penguins, coached Buffalo Sabres and New York Islanders. He has said: "I owe my whole hockey career to the Little NHL because, at the time when I played, I wasn't very welcomed anywhere else."
- **50th anniversary:** Celebrated in 2024 with record participation
- **Sponsors:** Hydro One is a major sponsor (https://www.hydroone.com/about/indigenous-relations/little-nhl)
