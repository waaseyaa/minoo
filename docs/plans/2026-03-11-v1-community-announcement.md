# Minoo V1 — Community Announcement

*Template for community leaders, band councils, and partner organizations.*

---

**Subject:** Minoo V1 is live — a platform for our communities

---

Aaniin,

We are sharing Minoo V1 — a platform built by a First Nations developer in northern Ontario to help our communities connect, preserve language and teachings, and care for our Elders.

**What Minoo does:**

- **Elder Support.** Elders or their families can request help — rides, groceries, visits. Volunteers sign up and get matched by proximity and skills. Coordinators manage everything through a simple dashboard.

- **Community Directory.** 637 First Nations communities with leadership and band office information. Find communities near you.

- **Language.** Anishinaabemowin dictionary entries, example sentences, and speaker information — preserving our language in a living, searchable format.

- **Teachings & Events.** Browse cultural teachings and find community events happening nearby.

**What Minoo does not do:**

- We do not sell, mine, or share community data with anyone.
- We do not use community knowledge to train AI models.
- We do not charge for access. All community content is shared under [CC BY-NC-SA 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-sa/4.0/).

**Content Attribution:**

Dictionary content displayed on Minoo is **copyrighted by The Ojibwe People's Dictionary** ([ojibwe.lib.umn.edu](http://ojibwe.lib.umn.edu)) and used under CC BY-NC-SA 4.0. We are grateful for their work in making Anishinaabemowin accessible.

**What comes next:**

Minoo is a living project. Community governance must lead the way on questions about data ownership, what content is public or restricted, and how language data is shared. We have built the platform to enforce whatever communities decide. The technology will not be the bottleneck.

We welcome feedback from community leaders, Knowledge Keepers, and anyone who wants to help shape what this becomes.

Miigwech,
The Minoo Project
